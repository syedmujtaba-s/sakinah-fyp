Sakinah v1.0.6 — the app notices when your words don't match your mood.

New:

1. Emotion mismatch check.
   If you check in feeling one way but then write a reflection that
   clearly says otherwise — e.g. you pick "Happy" but pour out something
   heavy and sad — Sakinah now gently asks: "You checked in feeling
   happy, but your reflection reads more like sad. Which feels truer
   right now?"

   You always have the final say:
   - "Keep Happy" → trusts your original check-in
   - "Switch to Sad" → updates your mood

   Whichever you pick is used for the guidance, saved with your journal
   entry, and shown on the guidance screen — so your Seerah story and
   your mood history actually match how you feel.

   Only strong contradictions trigger this — mild nuance won't. And it
   never interrupts a crisis: if your words signal real distress, the
   helpline response always comes first.

Carries forward:
- v1.0.5 customisable reminder times + day-of-week control
- v1.0.4 notification reliability fixes
- v1.0.2 off-topic detector

If anything feels off, the Send Feedback screen reaches the admin panel.
