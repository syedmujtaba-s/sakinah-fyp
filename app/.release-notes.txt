Sakinah v1.0.4 — notifications & reminders reliability fix.

Fixed (the "my reminders don't work" cluster):

1. Notification small-icon is now a proper white silhouette (heart).
   Previously the launcher icon was used, which Android renders as a
   white square. Now notifications look like Sakinah notifications.

2. Reminders fire on time even overnight. The scheduling mode changed
   from inexact to exactAllowWhileIdle so Doze mode doesn't delay them.
   Falls back to inexact mode if exact-alarm permission isn't granted
   on Android 12+ devices.

3. ScheduledNotificationReceiver explicitly declared in AndroidManifest
   as a safety net in case manifest merger ever drops it.

4. Reminder toggles now reflect reality:
   - Toggle ON only flips after scheduling actually succeeds.
   - If scheduling fails (denied permission, plugin error), the toggle
     stays OFF and you get a clear error snackbar instead of silently
     thinking it worked.

5. Habit reminders honour permission denial. Previously a habit with
   "Reminder: 8 PM" could be saved even when notifications were denied
   — the habit would appear in your list with the time visible but no
   notification ever fired. Now the habit saves without a reminder and
   tells you to enable notifications first.

6. NotificationService self-bootstraps. Schedule/cancel calls work even
   if main.dart's init failed silently (e.g. on a flaky first launch).

7. Notification channel explicitly created on Android 8+ with the right
   importance, so Samsung One UI devices don't drop reminders to a
   low-priority channel that never appears in the status bar.

New:

8. "Send test notification now" button at the bottom of the Reminders
   screen. Fires a notification immediately so you can verify the whole
   stack works without waiting for 8 PM.

Other:
- Carries v1.0.3's habit title fix and v1.0.2's off-topic detector.

If a reminder you set still doesn't fire after this update:
- Open the new Reminders screen and tap "Send test notification now"
  to verify the basics.
- If even the test doesn't fire, your device's "Alarms & reminders"
  permission is blocked. Open app settings → Permissions → enable.
